Burnout

Habits

Habits are the fourth piece of the puzzle, and I hope you realize by now that they are not the only piece Many of my patients and colleagues relied heavily on their habits to make up for a lack of the other pieces, and the point of this talk to highlight the risk of that approach Compare self care to a web application and review the importance of separation of concerns ask them if they have played pentiment or what games they play

Habits

Of all behaviors that have an impact on wellness, exercise (joyful movement) has the most impact. If 40% of health is dependent on consumer behaviors (which is a generous estimate acc to my research), and we've already spoken about primary medical care. (Notice that mental health care is not really mentioned here, so it's either bundled with regular health care or is heavily dependent on socioeconomic factors, otherwise it's mixed with behavioral) According to current population research, your daily habits have a maximum of 20% impact on your health. This could be discouraging, but it's meant to be a relief. It's not all up to you, and there are factors outside of your control.
