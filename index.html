Burnout

Burnout: the point at which the effort required to do something outweighs one's desire to do it.

According to a survey by the Bureau of Labor Statistics in 2021, the average software engineer turnover is 57.3 percent

Many experienced devs attribute this statistic to burnout rates, which haven't really been measured

If you're here, it's likely that you've had a direct encounter with burnout.

Recovery takes longer than prevention

Reasons for burnout: overstimulated or lack of diffuse time (biao), lack of self care (ben)

Habits

Of all behaviors that have an impact on wellness, exercise (joyful movement) has the most impact.

40% of health is dependent on consumer behaviors (a generous estimate). We've already spoken about primary medical care. (Notice that mental health care is not really mentioned here, so it's either bundled with regular health care or is heavily dependent on socioeconomic factors, otherwise it's mixed with behavioral)

According to current population research, your daily habits have a maximum of 20% impact on your health.

This could be discouraging, but it's meant to be a relief. It's not all up to you, and there are factors outside of your control.
